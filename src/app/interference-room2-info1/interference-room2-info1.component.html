• Constructive Interference – A type of interference that occurs at any
    location where the two interfering waves have a displacement in
    the same direction – Consequently the resultant wave has a
    displacement greater than both constituent waves.

  • Destructive Interference – A type of interference that occurs at any
    location where the two interfering waves have a displacement in
    the opposite directions. The result is that the final waveform has a
    displacement less than the constituent waves.
